Infinite Product Signature 5:10010
Slater Number S.18

Common name the first Rogers–Ramanujan identity

Product forms $$ \Pi(q):=\frac{(q^2,q^3,q^5;q^5)_\infty}{(q;q)_\infty} = \prod_{n=0}^\infty \frac{1}{(1-q^{5n+1})(1-q^{5n+4}) } = \frac{f(-q^2,-q^3)}{f(-q)} = i e^{-\pi i \tau/6} \frac{ \eta(5\tau) }{\vartheta_1(\tau \mid 5\tau)} = i e^{-\pi i 5\tau/6} \frac{ \vartheta_1(2\tau \mid 5\tau)}{\eta(\tau)} .$$

Identity $\Sigma(q) = \Pi(q)$ due to Rogers (1894, p. 328 (2)).

Bailey pair proof

Asymptotic formula for coefficient of $q^n$ $$ r(n) \sim \frac{1}{2\cdot 15^{1/4} n^{3/4}} \csc\left( \frac{\pi}{5} \right) \exp \left( 2\pi \sqrt{ \frac{n}{15}} \right) $$ \[ r(n) \sim \frac{e^{\frac{2 \pi \sqrt{n}}{\sqrt{15}}}}{4 \sqrt[4]{15} \sqrt{\frac{5}{8}-\frac{\sqrt{5}}{8}} n^{3/4}} \] as $n\to\infty$. Due to Lehner (1941, p. 655, Eq. (12.4)).

Combinatorial interpretations of $r(n)$

  • the number of partitions of $n$ into parts congruent to $\pm 1\pmod{5}$
  • the number of partitions of $n$ into parts that mutually differ by at least $2$.
