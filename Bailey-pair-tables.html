Definitions and Preliminaries

Bailey pair tables

A pair \( (\alpha_n, \beta_n) \) is called a Bailey pair relative to \( x/q \) if \[ \beta_n = \sum_{r=0}^n \frac{\alpha_r}{(x;q)_{n+r} (q;q)_{n-r}} \tag{BP} .\] In each case, Eq. (BP) will be evaluated using a specialization of Bailey's sum of a very-well-poised \({}_6\psi_6\) series (Bailey, 1935); see also Gasper and Rahman (2004, p. 140 ff.) \[ \sum_{n=-\infty}^\infty \frac{(1-aq^{2n})(b,c,d,e;q)_n} {(1-a)(aq/b, aq/c, aq/d, aq/e;q)_n } \Big( \frac{a^2q}{bcd} \Big)^n = \frac{(q,q/a,aq,aq/(bc),aq/(bd),aq/(be), aq/(cd), aq/(ce),aq/(de),q)_\infty} {(q/b,q/c,q/d,q/e,aq/b,aq/c,aq/d,aq/e,a^2q/(bcde);q)_\infty}, \tag{BBS}\] where \( |a^2 q / (bcde)| < 1 \).

Always, \( \alpha_0 = 1 . \)
