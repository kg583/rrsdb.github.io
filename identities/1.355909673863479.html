Article for infinite product with signature 4:(0,1,0,0)

\[ F(q) := \sum_{n=0}^\infty r(n)q^n := \frac{f(-q,-q^3)}{f(-q)} = \frac{1}{(q^2;q^4)_\infty}\]

Combinatorial interpretation of product

  • \(r(n)\) is the number of partitions of \(n\) with only parts congruent to 2 modulo 4.

  • \(r(n)\) is the number of partitions of \(n\) with distinct, even parts.

Asymptotic formula for \(r(n)\)

\(\displaystyle r(n) \sim \frac{e^{\frac{\pi \sqrt{n}}{\sqrt{6}}}}{4\sqrt[4]{6} n^{3/4}}\) as \( n\to\infty. \)
