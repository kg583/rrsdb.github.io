Rogers–Ramanujan type identities

Article for infinite product with signature 5:(1,0,0,1,0)

\[ G(q) := \sum_{n=0}^\infty r(n)q^n := \frac{f(-q^2,-q^3)}{f(-q)} = \frac{1}{(q, q^4; q^5)_\infty} = \prod_{n=1}^\infty \frac{1}{(1-q^{5n-1})(1-q^{5n-4})}\]

Combinatorial interpretation of product

\(r(n)\) is the number of partitions of \(n\) with parts congruent to \(\pm 1\) modulo \(5\).

Power series expansion

First terms:

\[1+q+q^2+q^3+2q^4+2q^5+3q^6+3q^7+4q^8+5q^9+6q^{10}+7q^{11}+9q^{12} +10q^{13}+12q^{14}+14q^{15}+17q^{16}+19q^{17}+23q^{18}+26q^{19}\cdots\]

Asymptotic formula for \(r(n)\)

\(\displaystyle r(n) \sim \frac{e^{\frac{2 \pi \sqrt{n}}{\sqrt{15}}}}{4 \sqrt[4]{15} \sqrt{\frac{5}{8}-\frac{\sqrt{5}}{8}} n^{3/4}}\) as \( n\to\infty. \)

\(q\)-series representations

\(\displaystyle G(q) = \sum_{n=0}^\infty \frac{q^{n^2}}{(q;q)_n}\)

Common name: The first Rogers-Ramanujan identity.

Bailey pair proof: Insert Bailey pair B(1) into the p-Bailey lemma and apply the triple product identity.

Combinatorial interpretations:

  • \(r(n)\) counts the number of partitions of \(n\) into parts that mutually differ by at least 2.

  • \(r(n)\) counts the number of partitions of \(n\) with one successive Durfee square.

History: Appears on Slater's list with Slater number 18.
