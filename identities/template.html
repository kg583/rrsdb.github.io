Rogers–Ramanujan type identities

Product signature 5:10001

\[ G(q) = \frac{f(-q^2,-q^3)}{f(-q)} = \frac{1}{(q, q^4; q^5)_\infty} = \prod_{n=1}^\infty \frac{1}{(1-q^{5n-1})(1-q^{5n-4})}\]

Series expansion with the first terms:

\[1+q+q^2+q^3+2q^4+2q^5+3q^6+3q^7+4q^8+5q^9+6+6q^{10}+7q^{11}+9q^{12} +10q^{13}+12q^{14}+14q^{15}+17q^{16}+19q^{17}+23q^{18}+26q^{19}\cdots\]

Combinatorial interpretation: Let \(r(n)\) be the coefficient of \(q^n\) in the series expansion. Then \(r(n)\) is the number of partitions of \(n\) with parts congruent to \(\pm 1\) modulo \(5\).

Representations as series

\[\sum_{n=0}^\infty \frac{q^{n^2}}{(q;q)_n}\]

Common name: The first Rogers-Ramanujan identity. Slater number: 18.

Bailey pair proof: Insert Bailey pair B(1) into the p-Bailey lemma and apply the triple product identity.

Combinatorial interpretation: \(r(n)\) counts the number of partitions of \(n\) into parts that mutually differ by at least 2.
